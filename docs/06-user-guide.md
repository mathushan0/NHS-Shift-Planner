## 1. Getting started

When you open the app for the first time, a short setup screen walks you through three steps:

1. **Your details** — enter your name, NHS trust, job role, and contracted weekly hours. These help the app calculate whether you're over or under your contracted hours. You can skip this and fill it in later.

2. **Notification permissions** — the app will ask permission to send you shift reminders. Tap **Allow** to enable this. If you skip it, you can enable notifications later in your device settings.

3. **You're ready** — your app is set up. No account or login needed.

> **No login required.** All your shift data is stored directly on your phone. You can use the app indefinitely without creating an account. Cloud backup is optional and available any time.

## 2. Adding a shift

### From the Dashboard (Home screen)

Tap the **+** button in the bottom-right corner of the screen.

### From the Calendar

Tap any date on the calendar, then tap the **+** button.

### Filling in the shift details

| Field | What to enter |
|-------|--------------|
| **Shift type** | Choose from your shift types (e.g. Long Day, Night, Short Day). These are colour-coded. |
| **Date** | The date the shift starts. |
| **Start time** | When your shift begins. |
| **End time** | When your shift ends. For overnight shifts, just enter the correct end time — the app handles midnight crossings automatically. |
| **Location** | Optional. Ward name, hospital site, etc. |
| **Notes** | Optional. Anything you want to note (e.g. "covering for Sarah"). |
| **Bank shift** | Toggle on if this is a bank or agency shift. Bank hours are tracked separately. |
| **Reminders** | Set one or more reminders before the shift starts. See [Setting up reminders](#5-setting-up-reminders). |

Tap **Save** when you're done.

### Overlap warning

If a shift overlaps with one you've already entered, the app will warn you. You can still save it — the warning is a nudge, not a block.

## 3. Editing or deleting a shift

### To edit a shift

1. Tap the shift on the Dashboard or Calendar to open the **Shift Detail** screen.
2. Tap **Edit** (top-right corner).
3. Make your changes and tap **Save**.

When you edit a shift, any reminders are automatically rescheduled.

### To delete a shift

1. Open the shift detail.
2. Tap **Delete**.
3. A message appears at the bottom of the screen with an **Undo** option. You have 5 seconds to undo before the shift is permanently removed.

## 4. Understanding the calendar

The **Calendar** tab shows your shifts across the month.

### What you'll see

- **Colour-coded dots** under each date — each dot represents a shift, coloured by shift type.
- **Bank holidays** are highlighted — based on your region (England & Wales, Scotland, or Northern Ireland).
- **Today** is marked with a circle.

### Navigating

- Swipe left or right to move between months.
- Tap any date to see the shifts on that day below the calendar.
- Tap a shift in the list to view its full details.

### Shift types and colours

The default shift types and their colours are:

| Shift type | Default colour |
|-----------|---------------|
| Long Day | NHS Blue |
| Night | Dark Blue |
| Short Day / Early | Light Blue |
| Late | Grey |
| Annual Leave | Aqua Green |
| Sick | Red |
| Rest Day | Pale Grey |

You can change these colours in Settings → Shift Types.

## 5. Setting up reminders

Reminders are push notifications sent to your phone before a shift starts.

### Default reminder

The app sets a reminder 2 hours before each shift by default. You can change this default in **Settings → Notifications**.

### Adding reminders when creating a shift

On the Add/Edit Shift screen, you'll see reminder options:

- **15 minutes before**
- **30 minutes before**
- **1 hour before**
- **2 hours before**

You can select more than one. For example, set both a 2-hour reminder and a 30-minute reminder if you want two nudges.

### If notifications aren't working

If you see a yellow banner on the Dashboard that says notifications are disabled:

1. Tap the banner — it will take you directly to your device's notification settings.
2. Find **MyShifts** in the list and turn notifications **On**.
3. Return to the app — the banner will disappear.

**Android tip:** Some Android phones (Samsung, Huawei, etc.) aggressively kill background apps to save battery. If your reminders aren't arriving reliably, go to your phone's battery settings and disable battery optimisation for MyShifts.

## 6. Checking your hours

Tap the **Hours** tab to see a summary of your worked hours.

### What's shown

- **Total hours** for the current pay period
- **Bank hours** tracked separately (so you can see them clearly)
- **Breakdown by shift type** — a visual chart showing how your hours are split
- **Contracted vs worked** — if you've entered your contracted weekly hours during setup, the app compares them to your worked hours

### Changing the pay period

NHS pay periods vary — weekly, fortnightly, or monthly. Set yours in **Settings → Pay Period**.

- **Weekly** — Monday to Sunday (or your chosen start day)
- **Fortnightly** — two-week blocks
- **Monthly (4-week)** — common for AfC-banded staff
- **Monthly (calendar)** — 1st to last day of the month

You can also change which day your pay period starts (e.g. some trusts start on a Wednesday).

### Hours counting rules

- Hours from `completed`, `in_progress`, and `scheduled` shifts are included.
- Cancelled and swapped-out shifts are excluded.
- Sick leave and annual leave: counted if the shift type is marked as paid (the defaults are).

## 7. Exporting your shifts

You can export your shifts as a PDF — useful for pay queries, bank shift invoicing, or personal records.

### How to export

1. Go to **Settings** (the More tab).
2. Tap **Export Shifts to PDF**.
3. Choose a date range.
4. The app generates a PDF and opens the share sheet — you can save it to Files, email it, or send it via WhatsApp.

The PDF includes shift type, date, start/end time, duration, location, and status.

## 8. Using the app offline

MyShifts is built to work without an internet connection. Everything — adding shifts, viewing your calendar, checking hours, setting reminders — works with no network.

### What happens when you're offline

- A small banner appears at the top of the screen letting you know you're offline.
- All your data is on your phone, so nothing is affected.
- If you have cloud sync enabled, any changes you make are queued and will sync automatically when you reconnect.

### Your data stays on your device

Even if you never set up cloud sync, your data is safely stored on your phone's local storage. It persists across app restarts and updates.

## 9. Setting up cloud backup

Cloud backup is optional. It lets you:
- Restore your shifts if you get a new phone
- View your shifts on a computer (via the web companion)

### How to set it up

1. Go to **Settings → Cloud Sync**.
2. Tap **Enable Cloud Sync**.
3. Create a free account with your email address.
4. Your shifts will sync automatically whenever you're online.

### What syncs

Everything: shifts, shift types, reminders, and settings.

### Restoring on a new phone

1. Install the app on your new phone.
2. Go through the onboarding and skip the setup for now.
3. Go to **Settings → Cloud Sync → Sign In**.
4. Sign in with your account.
5. Tap **Restore from Cloud** — your shifts will download.

### Security

Your data is stored in a European data centre. You are the only person who can see your shifts — the system uses row-level security to ensure that. Your email is never shared with third parties.

To delete your account and all cloud data: go to **Settings → Account → Delete Account**. Your local data on the phone is not affected.

## 10. Customising the app

### Dark mode

Go to **Settings → Appearance**.

- **System default** — follows your phone's dark/light mode setting
- **Always dark** — dark mode regardless of phone setting
- **Always light** — light mode regardless of phone setting

### App colours

You can change the app's primary colour to suit your preference. Go to **Settings → Appearance → Primary Colour**.

### Shift type colours

Each shift type has a colour used in the calendar and charts. To change a colour:

1. Go to **Settings → Shift Types**.
2. Tap the shift type you want to edit.
3. Choose a new colour.

### Adding custom shift types

If your trust uses shift names not in the default list (e.g. "Twilight", "Weekend Enhanced"):

1. Go to **Settings → Shift Types**.
2. Tap **+ Add Shift Type**.
3. Give it a name, colour, and default duration.
4. Toggle **Paid shift** on or off.

### Bank holiday region

The app shows UK bank holidays on your calendar. By default this is England & Wales. To change:

1. Go to **Settings → General**.
2. Tap **Bank Holiday Region**.
3. Choose **England & Wales**, **Scotland**, or **Northern Ireland**.

## 11. Tips for NHS staff

**Bank shifts:** Toggle the "Bank shift" option when adding a bank or agency shift. The Hours screen shows bank hours separately so you can track them independently from your contracted hours.

**Night shifts:** The app fully supports shifts that cross midnight. Just enter the correct start and end times (e.g. 19:30 to 07:30) and it calculates the duration correctly.

**Swapping shifts:** If you swap a shift, change its status to `Swapped Out`. The shift stays in your history (useful for records) but isn't counted in your hours. If you pick up someone else's shift, add it as `Swapped In`.

**Sick days:** Add a shift as normal and change the status to `Sick`. This logs it in your records — useful if you need to refer back.

**Annual leave:** Add your leave days as shifts using the Annual Leave shift type. They'll appear in your calendar and hours summary.

**Pay queries:** Use the PDF export to produce a record of your worked shifts. The export includes start/end times and durations — exactly what you need for a pay query.
